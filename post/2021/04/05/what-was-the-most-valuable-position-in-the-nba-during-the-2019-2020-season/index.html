What was the most valuable position in the NBA during the 2019-2020 season?

Joe Wright / 2021-04-05

One of the longest held debates amongst NBA fanatics is over which player deserves the Most Valuable Player (MVP) award for each given season. It is a purposefully subjective award that stirs the pot and incites heated arguments between both media members and fans about what value really means in the NBA.

While this debate is a fascinating discussion, I want to look at player value in a broader sense. What I want to find out is what is the most valuable position in the NBA? Which position is responsible for the most wins, which are the least replaceable and which ones don’t have as much of an impact?

While I would love to analyze positional value in the NBA for as long as the league has been active, I simply don’t have the means of acquiring decades of player statistics. Instead, I concentrated my focus on the 2019-2020 NBA season. Looking at the past season’s data allows me to also investigate another media talking point: the death of the center position. Small ball is all the rage… or is it?

Let’s go ahead and find out!
